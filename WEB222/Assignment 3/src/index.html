Discover the Majesty of Takhte Jamshid

Step into the heart of ancient Persia and walk where kings once stood.

Historical Information

Takhte Jamshid, also known as Persepolis, was founded by Darius I in 518 BCE and served as the ceremonial capital of the Achaemenid Empire. The name “Takhte Jamshid” means “Throne of Jamshid,” referring to a legendary king. Built from limestone and adorned with intricate carvings, this ancient city symbolized the power and artistry of Persia’s golden age. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most treasured archaeological marvels in the Middle East.

How to Get There

Persepolis lies near Shiraz within Iran's Fars Province. One can drive there from the Shiraz International Airport and it usually takes about an hour. It can be approached by tour bus, taxi, or personal car. One can get guides at the gate and these are highly recommended for a more participatory experience.

Local Attractions

Besides Persepolis itself, nearby landmarks include Naqsh-e Rustam (an ancient necropolis) and Pasargadae, the tomb of Cyrus the Great. In Shiraz, don't miss the Pink Mosque, Eram Garden, and the tombs of poets Hafez and Saadi.

Popular Folklore

Persian legends say King Jamshid built the city and ruled during a golden age though this is myth the stories add cultural richness to the ruins Many believe the site carries spiritual energy tied to Nowruz, the Persian New Year.

Where to Stay & Eat

Shiraz has hotels from budget to luxury ttry traditional Persian dishes like fesenjan, kebab koobideh, and ghormeh sabzi in restaurants like Haft Khan and Shater Abbas.
